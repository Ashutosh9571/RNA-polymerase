RNA Polymerase

RNA polymerase is an essential enzyme found in all organisms that synthesizes RNA from a DNA template. It plays a crucial role in the process of transcription, where genetic information from DNA is copied into RNA.

During transcription, RNA polymerase binds to a specific DNA sequence called a promoter, unwinds the DNA strands, and assembles RNA nucleotides into a complementary RNA strand. This process is fundamental for gene expression.

There are different types of RNA polymerase in eukaryotic cells: RNA polymerase I, II, and III, each responsible for transcribing different types of RNA. In prokaryotes, a single type of RNA polymerase performs all RNA synthesis tasks.

RNA polymerase is a complex protein with multiple subunits that work together to ensure high fidelity and efficiency during RNA synthesis. Its regulation is critical for controlling gene expression in response to cellular and environmental signals.
